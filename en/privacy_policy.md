# SOCIALO Privacy Policy

## 1. INTRODUCTION

At SOCIALO we work to offer the user the best possible experience through our mobile application. In some cases, it is necessary to collect information to achieve this.

Therefore, and for the purposes of REGULATION (EU) 2016/679 OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL of 27 April 2016 (hereinafter, "GDPR") relating to the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and the free movement of such data, and Organic Law 3/2018, of 5 December, on the Protection of Personal Data and guarantee of digital rights (hereinafter, "LOPDGDD"), SOCIALO, S.L. informs the user about the processing of their personal data in accordance with this policy.

This privacy policy is available both in the application itself and in the app stores (App Store, Google Play), so you can consult it before installing the application or at any time during its use.

## 3. COLLECTION OF PERSONAL DATA AND ITS PROCESSING

### 3.1. Categories of Personal Data

Below are the categories of personal data that may be processed in the application, to the extent that your organization enables them:

**a) Identification data:** Name, surnames, identity document (DNI, NIE, passport, or other identification document), photograph.

**b) Contact data:** Email (personal and institutional), mobile and landline phone number, postal address, professional social media profiles.

**c) Organizational context data:** Academic, professional, or context information relevant to the community (for example: university degree, institution, department, professional area, specialization, position, function, or role within the organization).

**d) Location or assignment data:** Location, assigned space, or membership information within the organization (for example: room number, building, office, area, section, group, team, or unit), incorporation and expected end dates when applicable.

**e) Participation and activity data:** Activities in which you participate, events you attend, activity preferences, declared personal interests, groups or committees you belong to, comments and ratings.

**f) User profile data:** Personal biography, interests, hobbies, languages, skills, notification and communication preferences.

**g) Platform usage data:** Access logs, connection date and time, pages visited within the platform, content interactions.

### 3.2. Processing Purposes

Personal data will be processed for the following purposes:

- **User management and access control:** Registration, signup, and platform access.
- **Internal communication:** Facilitating communication between community members and with the organization's administration.
- **Activity organization:** Organization and promotion of activities, events, and services of the organization.
- **Community participation:** Improving member participation and engagement in the community.
- **Notifications:** Sending notifications and communications related to the service.
- **Maintenance and support:** Platform maintenance, technical support, and improvement.
- **Statistics:** Preparation of aggregate and anonymized statistics on platform usage.
- **User support:** Query management and support.

### 3.3. Special Categories of Data (Sensitive Data)

SOCIALO **DOES NOT process special categories of personal data** under any circumstances pursuant to Article 9 of the GDPR, which include:

- Data revealing racial or ethnic origin.
- Political opinions.
- Religious or philosophical beliefs.
- Trade union membership.
- Genetic data.
- Biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person.
- Data concerning physical or mental health.
- Data concerning sex life or sexual orientation.

Data relating to criminal convictions and offences pursuant to Article 10 of the GDPR is also not processed.

If the processing of these categories of data is accidentally detected, it will be immediately deleted and the data controller will be notified to take appropriate corrective measures.

## 4. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING YOUR DATA

The legal basis justifying the processing of the aforementioned data is:

- **Contract performance** for registration, access, and use of the application.
- **Consent of the data subject** for sending commercial communications, data visibility in the community, and optional cookies.
- **Legitimate interest** for service analysis and improvement.
- **Compliance with legal obligations** for communication with public authorities and responding to legal requirements.

## 5. RETENTION OF YOUR PERSONAL DATA

Personal data is only kept for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, to meet your needs, or to comply with legal obligations.

### 5.1. Retention Periods

- **Account data:** While the account is active and you maintain your relationship with the organization.
- **Activity data:** For the time necessary to manage the activity and, subsequently, during the applicable statute of limitations periods.
- **Data for legal obligations:** During the legally established periods according to applicable regulations.

### 5.2. End of Relationship

When your relationship with the organization ends or your account is deleted:

- You will have a period to request the download of your data through the established channels.
- Data will be deleted or, where appropriate, returned to the data controller (your organization).

### 5.3. Blocking Period

Pursuant to Article 32 of the LOPDGDD, personal data may be kept **duly blocked** for a maximum period of **5 years** from the end of the relationship, exclusively to address possible legal liabilities arising from the processing (tax, civil, commercial, or administrative), in compliance with Article 1.964.2 of the Spanish Civil Code.

During the blocking period, data **may not be accessed or processed**, except to make it available to:
- Courts and Tribunals
- The Public Prosecutor's Office
- Competent Public Administrations
- The Ombudsman
- The Court of Auditors
- The Spanish Data Protection Agency or other data protection authorities

### 5.4. Final Deletion

Once the blocking period has elapsed without requiring retention, data will be definitively deleted through secure procedures that prevent recovery, or anonymized so that the user can no longer be identified.

## 8. EXERCISE OF RIGHTS AND HOW YOU CAN EXERCISE THEM

### 8.1. How to Exercise Your Rights

If you are an **end user** (student, resident, member), you can exercise your rights:
- By contacting the data controller of your organization directly.
- Through dpo@socialo.live, who will forward your request to the corresponding controller immediately and, in any case, within a maximum of one business day.

If you are an **organization administrator**, you can direct your communications to: dpo@socialo.live

### 8.2. Rights Recognized by the GDPR

Under the GDPR (Articles 15 to 22) you can exercise the following rights:

- **Right of access (Art. 15):** You can request information about whether your personal data is being processed and, if so, obtain a copy thereof and information about the processing.

- **Right to rectification (Art. 16):** You can request the correction of inaccurate personal data or complete incomplete data.

- **Right to erasure / "Right to be forgotten" (Art. 17):** You can request the deletion of your personal data when, among other reasons, it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.

- **Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18):** You can request that the processing of your personal data be restricted in certain circumstances.

- **Right to data portability (Art. 20):** You can request to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (for example: JSON, CSV, XML), and transmit it to another controller.

- **Right to object (Art. 21):** You can object to the processing of your personal data, including profiling.

- **Right not to be subject to automated individual decision-making (Art. 22):** You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects you. SOCIALO does not make automated decisions of this type without human intervention.

### 8.3. Response Time

Requests for exercise of rights will be addressed within a maximum period of **one month** from receipt. This period may be extended by another two months in case of complex or numerous requests, informing you of such extension within one month of receipt of the request.

### 8.4. Limitations

In some cases, the request may be denied if you request the deletion of data necessary for:
- Compliance with legal obligations.
- The establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.
- Reasons of public interest in the field of public health.

### 8.5. Complaint to the Supervisory Authority

If you consider that your rights have not been properly addressed, you have the right to file a complaint with the **Spanish Data Protection Agency (AEPD)**:

## 10. SECURITY MEASURES

SOCIALO has adopted the necessary technical and organizational security measures to guarantee the protection of personal data, pursuant to Article 32 of the GDPR. These measures allow:

- **Confidentiality:** Ensuring that only authorized persons have access to personal data.
- **Integrity:** Ensuring that personal data is not altered without authorization.
- **Availability:** Ensuring that personal data is available when needed.
- **Resilience:** Ability of systems to recover from physical or technical incidents.

### 10.1. Technical Measures Implemented

- **Encryption in transit:** The application uses secure connections with TLS certificate, encrypting all transmitted information using HTTPS protocol.
- **Encryption at rest:** Stored data is protected using encryption techniques.
- **Pseudonymization:** When possible, pseudonymization techniques are applied to reduce risks.
- **Access control:** Authentication and authorization systems to limit data access.
- **Backups:** Regular backup procedures to ensure data recovery.
- **Monitoring:** Security incident detection and response systems.

### 10.2. Organizational Measures

- **Training:** Personnel who access personal data receive specific training in data protection.
- **Confidentiality:** All personnel are subject to written confidentiality commitments.
- **Periodic evaluations:** Regular verifications and evaluations of the effectiveness of implemented security measures are carried out.

### 10.3. Security Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data security breach that could pose a high risk to your rights and freedoms, you will be informed without undue delay, pursuant to Article 34 of the GDPR, so that you can take necessary precautions.

## 11. PROCESSING OF MINORS' DATA

### 11.1. Minimum Age

Pursuant to Article 7 of the LOPDGDD, the processing of minors' personal data may only be based on their consent when they are over **14 years old**.

### 11.2. Minors Under 14

The processing of personal data of minors under 14 will require the consent of the holder of parental authority or guardianship. In these cases, the organization (data controller) must verify that consent has been given by the holder of parental authority or guardianship over the minor.

### 11.3. Organization Responsibility

It is the organization's (data controller's) responsibility to ensure that age and consent requirements are met for processing data of minors using the platform.
